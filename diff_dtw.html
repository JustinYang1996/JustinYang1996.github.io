Alleviating Discontinuity Effects on Direct Waveform Modification for Voice Conversion Synthesis

This is a demo page for Alleviating Discontinuity Effects on Direct Waveform Modification for Voice Conversion Synthesis

Abstract

Current exemplar-based Voice Conversion (VC) methods require parallel speech data at the online/training stage. However, in most real-life situations, the source and target speaker usually do not speak the same sentence or even speak different languages. Hence, developing algorithms for non-parallel cases would be required and more suitable for real-life applications. This paper describes an exemplar-based many-to-one voice conversion method via Locally Linear Embedding (LLE) for non-parallel corpora, utilizing pre-softmaxed Phonetic PosteriorGram (PPG), which is mostly speaker independent but partially speaker dependent, to perform conversion on voice.

Dataset Used

We evaluated our proposed framework on the Voice Conversion Challenge 2018 (VCC 2018) dataset. [Paper][Dataset]

Method

We proposed a Exemplar-based Voice Conversion framework utilizing manifold learning algorithm Locally Linear Embedding to perform conversion. Specifically, we applied Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) techniques (pre-softmaxed Phonetic Posteriorgram (psPPG) obtained by ASR) to further improve our perfromance on non-parallel cases. To be more specific, we utilize psPPG as an intermidiate feature to perform conversion on mel cepstrum coefficients derived from STRAIGHT-vocoded spectrum and synthesizes waveform with the STRAIGHT Vocoder.

Worth of mentioning, there's no need for alignment (DTW) at the offline stage, since only target information is needed.
